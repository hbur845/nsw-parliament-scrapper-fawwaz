Community Preschools

Mr MARK SPEAKMAN (Cronulla) (11:12:29): My question is directed to the Premier. Community preschool educators join us in the gallery today. They earn up to 30 per cent less than teachers in schools doing similar work, and around 15 per cent less than staff in long day care. Families trust their children with these people every day. Why will the Government not fund a fair pay rise for the incredible work they do?

The SPEAKER: Members will come to order.

Mr CHRIS MINNS (Kogarah—Premier) (11:13:08): That concern from the industry would have been welcomed when the Opposition was in government. The truth is that over the 12 years that the previous Government was in power there was record low investment in that area of public spending. The former Liberal‑Nationals Government did not build a single public preschool in New South Wales in 12 years. As a result, there is a massive differential between the number of private providers and public and community providers in Victoria versus New South Wales. Most of the childcare deserts in our State are funded by private providers because there are no public providers. As a result, the Government has stepped into the breach and announced the largest early childhood education investment, which includes 100 new preschools in the State, 49 of which will be in regional New South Wales. The reason for that is straightforward.

Ms Eleni Petinos: Point of order—

The SPEAKER: The Clerk will stop the clock.

Ms Eleni Petinos: My point of order relates to Standing Order 129. The Premier was asked about preschool workers' pay, not bricks and mortar.

The SPEAKER: There is no point of order. The Premier was being directly relevant. The Premier has the call.

Mr CHRIS MINNS: That goes to my point. Under the Coalition, there were no preschools to fund—none, zero—because its members did not build a single preschool. The community sector is underpaid.

The SPEAKER: Members will come to order.

Mr CHRIS MINNS: After 12 years of the former Government putting virtually nothing into the sector, this Government has made the decision to build 100 public preschools.

The SPEAKER: The Leader of the Opposition will come to order. I call the member for North Shore to order for the first time.

Mr CHRIS MINNS: We are working with the community sector and unions to provide pay at the appropriate levels.

The SPEAKER: I call the Leader of the Opposition to order for the first time.

Mr CHRIS MINNS: After three years in opposition the Coalition has offered nothing in the way of a policy prescription, yet its members ask, "Where have you been for the past three years?" I ask Opposition members where were they in the decade before that?

The SPEAKER: I will not tolerate members shouting across the Chamber after the Premier has resumed his seat. It is disorderly. Members will be respectful of each other in the Chamber.
